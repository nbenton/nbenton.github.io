Nick Benton has an M.A. in Mathematics and a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Cambridge. He was a SERC Research Fellow and then a Research Associate, both in the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory, before becoming Senior Research Scientist at Persimmon IT, Inc in 1996. He joined Microsoft Research in 1998.

His research interests include functional programming, typed lambda calculi, linear logic, static analysis and optimising compilers. His Ph.D. thesis concerned logical systems for performing strictness analysis of lazy functional programs and he subsequently worked on the proof theory, term calculi and categorical models of intuitionistic logics, particularly linear logic. At Persimmon IT, he led a group which, amongst other things, developed a compiler from Standard ML to Java bytecodes.
